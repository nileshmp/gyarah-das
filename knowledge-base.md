## About the programme

Gyarah Das supports mothers through pregnancy and the child's first 1000 days
(from pregnancy until the child turns two). It does not replace any service —
it helps each mother become aware of, ask for, and reach the health, nutrition,
and welfare services that already exist around her, at the right time.

## Nearby Health Facilities — Anwa

| Facility | Name | Distance | Travel time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary Health Centre (PHC) | PHC Saifni | 8–12 km | 15–25 min |
| Community Health Centre (CHC) | CHC Shahabad | 14–18 km | 25–35 min |
| District Hospital | District Hospital Rampur | 35–40 km | 60–75 min |

What each facility is for:
- **PHC Saifni (Primary Health Centre)** — the nearest government health centre
  with a doctor. Good for pregnancy check-ups, IFA and TT, normal deliveries, and
  first-level care. About 8–12 km away, 15–25 minutes.
- **CHC Shahabad (Community Health Centre)** — a larger centre with more staff and
  often specialists and emergency delivery care (including caesarean in many CHCs)
  and inpatient beds. About 14–18 km, 25–35 minutes.
- **District Hospital Rampur** — the largest facility nearby, with specialists,
  surgery, a sick-newborn care unit (SNCU/NICU), and a blood bank. Used for
  high-risk pregnancies, complications, and very sick newborns. About 35–40 km,
  60–75 minutes.

If a mother mentions one of these places (for example "Saifni" or "Shahabad"),
recognise it, and gently tell her what it is, how far it is, and what it can help
with.

## Government Schemes & Entitlements

Explain these simply, only when relevant to her stage or when she asks.

- **Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY)** — cash assistance to encourage delivery in a
  government or accredited facility (institutional delivery).
- **Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY)** — a maternity benefit that gives
  cash support to pregnant and lactating mothers (conditional on registration and
  check-ups).
- **Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK)** — free delivery (including caesarean),
  free medicines, tests, diet, and transport for pregnant women and sick newborns
  at government facilities.
- **Take Home Ration (THR) / ICDS Anganwadi** — supplementary nutrition given
  through the Anganwadi centre, plus growth monitoring and counselling.
- **Iron and Folic Acid (IFA) tablets** — free tablets to prevent anaemia and
  support the baby's healthy growth.
- **Immunization** — the TT injection protects mother and baby during pregnancy;
  the child then follows the routine immunization schedule.

## Emergency Helplines

- **102** — free transport for pregnant women and children (pickup from home to a
  facility and back).
- **108** — emergency medical ambulance for any emergency.

These three-digit numbers are public and may be shared freely.

## The 1000-Day Quarter Framework

Pregnancy:
- **Q1** — First trimester (0–3 months)
- **Q2** — Second trimester (4–6 months)
- **Q3** — Third trimester (7–9 months)

After birth (child age):
- **Q4** — Birth to 3 months (0–90 days)
- **Q5** — 4–6 months (91–180 days)
- **Q6** — 7–9 months (181–270 days)
- **Q7** — 10–12 months (271–360 days)
- **Q8** — 13–15 months (361–450 days)
- **Q9** — 16–18 months (451–540 days)
- **Q10** — 19–21 months
- **Q11** — 22–24 months

New registration is allowed during pregnancy, or when a child is 18 months or
younger. Once registered, support continues until the child turns two.
